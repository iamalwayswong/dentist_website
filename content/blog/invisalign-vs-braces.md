It's the question we hear most often: should I go with traditional braces or Invisalign? Both are excellent treatments — the right choice depends on your case, your lifestyle, and your goals.

## The short answer

- **Choose braces** if you have a complex bite issue, want the lowest cost, or worry about compliance.
- **Choose Invisalign** if you want a discreet, removable option and have a mild-to-moderate case.

## How they compare

### Appearance

Braces are visible. Modern brackets are smaller than they were 20 years ago, and ceramic ("clear") brackets blend in much better than metal — but they're still noticeable.

Invisalign aligners are nearly invisible in everyday life. Most people won't know you're wearing them unless you tell them.

### Treatment time

For comparable cases, treatment times are similar — usually 12–24 months. Complex cases sometimes resolve faster with braces because the orthodontist has more precise control over individual teeth.

### Cost

Cost varies by case, but Invisalign and braces are typically priced within a few hundred dollars of each other. Insurance treats them the same way in most plans.

### Daily life

- **Braces** are on 24/7. No compliance required, but you'll need to avoid certain foods (popcorn kernels, hard candy, gum).
- **Invisalign** is removable. You can eat anything — but you need to wear the aligners 20–22 hours per day and remember to put them back in.

### Maintenance

Braces require slightly more careful brushing and flossing around brackets. Invisalign requires cleaning the aligners and brushing after every meal before reinserting.

## How to decide

The honest answer: come in for a free consultation. We'll do a quick exam and tell you what the orthodontist would recommend — and why. There's no pressure to start same-day, and we'll send you home with a clear written plan.

Both treatments produce beautiful results when they're the right fit. The job of a good orthodontist is to help you figure out which is right for *you*.
